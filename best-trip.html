🚌 Getting Around From Via Torricella

Your Airbnb is right off Via Torricella past Zi’Ntonio — here’s how to get everywhere

By bus: The SITA bus stop is at Zi’Ntonio, 1 min from your door. Buses go to Amalfi every 30–60 min (15 min ride, €1.30). From Amalfi you connect to Positano, Salerno, ferries to Capri, and the coast road. Buy tickets at any tabaccheria or the Amalfi bus station — not on the bus.

By foot to Ravello: Walk down Via Torricella toward the valley, then follow signs to Ravello. It’s about 12 min downhill on ancient stone paths. The walk back up is ~18 min and steep, but shaded. You can also take the bus from Scala Piazza to Ravello if your legs are tired.

By foot to Atrani/Amalfi: There’s a walking path from Scala down through the valley to Atrani (45–60 min, steep steps, beautiful). Or just take the bus — much easier.

By ferry (from Amalfi): Bus to Amalfi, then Travelmar ferries to Positano (€10, 25 min), Salerno (€10, 35 min), or Minori/Maiori (€5, 10 min). NLG ferries to Capri (€23, 80 min).

Best pass: 24-hr Unico Costiera (€10) = unlimited SITA buses. Worth it any day you take 3+ rides. Buy at Amalfi bus station or tabaccherie.

Driving? Don’t. The coast road (SS163) is a one-lane nightmare in August. Buses and ferries are the way. Your Airbnb has free parking nearby if you rent a car for a Pompeii day trip, but for coast travel, go public.
🛵 Food Delivery & Eating In

Can You Get Delivery in Scala?

Short answer: no app delivery. But you’ve got great options.

No delivery apps: Glovo, Just Eat, Deliveroo, and Uber Eats have zero coverage in Scala, Ravello, or Amalfi. These are tiny mountain villages with winding one-lane roads — none of the platforms operate here.

🍕 Takeaway pizza: Zi’Ntonio (1 min from your door!) does pizza da asporto — call or walk over, order, and bring it back to the terrace. Same with Sotto le Stelle and Il Pinguino — most local pizzerias will box it up for you. Just ask: “Pizza da portare via?”

🍳 Cook at home: The Airbnb kitchen has everything. Grab fresh bread, mozzarella, local tomatoes, pasta & wine at Mini Market Mostaccioli (8 min walk). A full dinner for two from the shop costs €10–15 — eat on the terrace with that sea view.

🍲 Homemade breakfast: The host Cristina makes incredible homemade breakfast items — guests rave about her fig & lemon jam, French toast, and fresh fruit from the garden. This is included and beats any café.

📞 Pro tip for Amalfi: If you’re down in Amalfi town, Pizza Express (Via Pietro Capuano 46, ★ 4.6) does great takeaway pizza & calzones for the bus ride home. Open 10:30 AM–late.

🍕 Food Cheat Sheet

Eating well without blowing the budget

Must-Try Dishes

The Amalfi Coast essentials

Scialatielli ai frutti di mare — fresh hand-rolled pasta with seafood, the coast’s signature dish
Delizia al limone — lemon sponge cake with limoncello cream, heavenly
Frittura di paranza — crispy fried mixed fish, best at beach restaurants
Insalata caprese — local mozzarella di bufala + tomatoes, nothing like at home
Sfogliatella — crispy shell-shaped pastry filled with ricotta, a Neapolitan staple
Limoncello — homemade everywhere, served ice-cold after dinner (free at many spots)

Budget hack: Eat pranzo (lunch) as your big meal — many restaurants offer fixed lunch menus at half the dinner price. For dinner, grab pizza al taglio or cook at the Airbnb. Alimentari shops have incredible cheese & salumi for €5–8.

💡 Quick Tips

SITA bus pass: Buy a 24-hr Unico Costiera pass (€10) for unlimited coast buses. Covers Scala–Amalfi, Amalfi–Positano, Amalfi–Ravello all in one. Get it at any tabaccheria.
Cash is king: Many small restaurants, buses, and beach vendors are cash-only. The Amalfi post office has an ATM (Postamat). Withdraw euros at Scotiabank before you fly for best rates, or use your Wise card.
Getting to Scala: From Naples Airport: Alibus to Napoli Centrale → train to Salerno → SITA bus to Amalfi → local bus up to Scala. Total ~3 hrs, ~€15pp. Private transfer cuts it to ~2 hrs for ~€130 flat.
Scala advantage: You’re above the chaos. The terrace at Windows on Heaven gives you a sunset view every single night without paying €15 for a spritz at a tourist bar. Ravello is a 10-min walk. Amalfi is a 15-min bus. Best of both worlds.
