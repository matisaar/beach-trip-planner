1
Athens — Glyfada · 2 nights
Aug 22–24 · Open in Maps

Skip the chaotic city centre. Glyfada is a chic coastal suburb in south Athens — beach bars, great restaurants, metro to the Acropolis. The perfect decompression stop after a long flight.

💡 Insider tip

Stay in Glyfada or south Athens. Rent an electric bike to explore the coast. Optional day trip to Cape Sounion — beautiful drive along the water with beaches, sunsets, and the Temple of Poseidon. Family there can show you around.

2
Mykonos · 3 nights
Aug 24–27 · Open in Maps

The iconic party island. Whitewashed streets, harbour windmills, world-class nightlife, and beautiful beaches. Expensive but worth it for 2–3 nights. Stay in Mykonos Town near the harbour — walk home, no taxis.

💡 Insider tip

Stay walking distance from the harbour — taxis are impossible at night and you’ll want to walk home after bars. Party at least one night. Go to Super Paradise Beach but avoid prime sun hours (the party starts at 4pm). Gyros are cheap (€3–4) — drinks are NOT. Windmill photos are a must.

3
Santorini · 2 nights
Aug 27–29 · Open in Maps

The caldera island. Breathtaking cliff-top villages, legendary sunsets, volcanic beaches. 2 nights is perfect — it’s stunning but crowded and expensive. Stay in Imerovigli (quieter, caldera views) or Oia (iconic but mobbed at sunset).

💡 Insider tip

Rent a 4-wheeler or ATV — taxis are IMPOSSIBLE to get, especially at sunset. Dinner in Fira is a MUST — the views are unreal. Visit wineries (Santorini wine is unique). The beaches suck compared to other islands (volcanic = black/red rock) — if you go to any beach, go to Red Beach. Great hiking between villages. Don’t stay more than 2 nights.

⚓ Getting to Crete

Option A (recommended): Ferry Santorini → Heraklion (~2–4 hrs, €40–60pp) → pick up rental car → drive to Chania (2.5 hrs on north coast highway). You need a car in Crete anyway!
Option B: Fly Santorini → Athens → Chania (~4 hrs total with connection, ~€80–120pp). More comfortable but pricier.

4
Chania, Crete · 4 nights
Aug 29–Sep 2 · Open in Maps

The grand finale. Crete is nothing like the other islands — it’s huge, wild, and has the best food in Greece. Chania’s Venetian harbour is magical. This is where you slow down, rent a car, and discover.

💡 Insider tip

You DEFINITELY need a rental car in Crete. Do the Samaria Gorge — it’s 16km, an organized trip, and absolutely breathtaking. Elafonissi has PINK SAND. Try Gramvousa restaurant — Niki’s all-time favourite. Cretan food is unique — amazing cheese, fresh everything. Just drive around and discover!

🔭 Optional Add-Ons — Milos & Serifos

If you have extra time (14+ days), slot these between Mykonos & Santorini. Both are quieter, less touristy gems.

🌊 Milos — 2–3 nights

White beaches, old fishing villages, incredibly relaxing. Rent a car to explore. Sarakiniko Beach (lunar white rock) · Kleftiko (sea caves, boat trip) · Plaka village (sunset). Ferry from Mykonos ~4–5 hrs or from Athens ~7 hrs.

💡 Golden Rules — from a Local

🔒
Leave your passport in the safe. Carry a photo of it on your phone. Don’t carry lots of cash.
👗
Dress nice everywhere. Greeks dress up even for casual dinners. No gym clothes at restaurants.
⚓
Book ferries on ferries.gr. Figure out your island order first, then plan boats/flights. Piraeus is the main port from Athens.
🚗
Rent cars in Santorini & Crete. ATV in Santorini (taxis impossible). Full car in Crete (essential for gorges & beaches).
🍴
Don’t eat at the first place you see. Check TripAdvisor reviews + check the map location. Harbour-front = tourist trap.
🚌
Public transit is cheap. Metro in Athens, ferries between islands, KTEL buses in Crete. Use them.
🌞
Don’t over-plan. Leave room to discover. Some of the best moments will be random.
🎉
Mykonos: Party at least one night. Walk-home distance from harbour is non-negotiable.
🌇
Santorini: 2 nights MAX. Fira dinner required. Beaches are volcanic (skip them or do Red Beach).
🥾
Crete: Samaria Gorge is a MUST. Elafonissi is a MUST. Rent a car. Eat everything.
