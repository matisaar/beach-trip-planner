🏛️ Your Neighbourhood — Chania Old Town

Venetian harbour, narrow lanes, everything on foot

Location: Your Airbnb is in the heart of the Venetian Old Town on Zambeliou Street — the most atmospheric lane in Chania. The harbour, restaurants, museums, and beaches are all walking distance. No car needed in town.

🏝️ Things to Do Within Walking Distance

Venetian Lighthouse Walk

10 min walk along the harbour wall

Walk the full length of the old harbour seawall to the iconic Venetian lighthouse. Best at sunset — the light on the harbour is unreal. Free. Go early to avoid crowds on the narrow path.

★ 4.7 · Free · Map

Maritime Museum of Crete

5 min walk — inside Firkas Fortress

Covers Cretan seafaring from Bronze Age to WWII. Small but excellent — the Battle of Crete exhibit is intense. Under an hour. A/C inside, great heat escape.

★ 4.5 · €4 · Map

Splantzia Quarter

3 min walk east of harbour

The bohemian neighbourhood just behind the harbour. Plane trees, hidden courtyards, artisan shops, and the best tavernas the tourists miss. Wander with no plan.

★ 4.6 · Free · Map

Nea Chora Beach

12 min walk west of Old Town

Small sandy beach with calm water. Local crowd, no tourist buses. Sunbeds €8/pair or lay a towel for free. Fish tavernas right on the sand — Pallas is the move.

★ 4.4 · Free · Map

🚌 Getting Around From Chania

You’ll need a car for 3 days, bus/foot for the rest

On foot: Chania Old Town is fully walkable. Everything in the neighbourhood section above is on foot. You don’t need a car for town days.

KTEL bus: KTEL bus station is a 5-min taxi from Old Town. Buses run to Rethymno (1 hr, €7), Omalos / Samaria Gorge (1 hr, €8), and Heraklion (2.5 hr, €15). Buy at the station or online at e-ktel.com.

Rental car (3 days): Essential for Balos, Elafonissi, and Seitan Limania. Buses exist but are slow and infrequent. Rent from the airport or Old Town pickup. ~€35–45/day in August. Book ahead on DiscoverCars.

Samaria Gorge day: Special KTEL bus package — bus to Omalos (6:15 AM), you hike down, ferry from Agia Roumeli to Hora Sfakion, bus back. No car needed — the road back is different from the start.

Airport: CHQ airport is 15 min by taxi (€25) or 30 min by city bus from KTEL station.

Driving on Crete: Roads are good but mountain roads are narrow and winding. Greeks drive fast. Park outside Old Town — there’s a lot at 1866 Square (€3/day). Don’t drive into the Old Town — it’s pedestrian.

📅 Day-by-Day Itinerary

Flexible vibes — swap any day around, nothing is locked

Day 1 — Arrival Day ✈️

Sat Aug 22 · Travel & settle in
🌅 Arrive

Land at Chania (CHQ). Taxi to Old Town (€25, 15 min) or pre-book a transfer. CHQ is tiny — you’ll be in a cab within 10 min of landing. Alternatively fly into Athens (ATH) and connect on Aegean Airlines to CHQ (45 min, ~€50–80).

🌱 Settle

Check into your Old Town apartment. Drop bags, grab a freddo espresso (€3) from any kafeneio. Walk to Agora Market for olive oil, tomatoes, feta, bread — breakfast sorted for the week.

🌙 Evening

Sunset walk along Venetian Harbour. The light on the water at dusk is otherworldly. First dinner at your 1-min-walk neighbour.

Tamam — creative Cretan in a converted Ottoman bathhouse. Order the lamb with stamnagathi (wild greens).
Map · ~€20pp

Day 2 — Chania Old Town 🏛️

Sun Aug 23 · Harbour, history & beach
☀️ Morning

Start at Bougatsa Iordanis — legendary custard pie (€3.50), the best breakfast in Crete. Then walk the full Venetian Harbour loop. Visit the Maritime Museum (€4) inside Firkas Fortress, then walk out to the Lighthouse.

🌊 Afternoon

Wander through Splantzia quarter. Browse the leather shops on Stivanadika street. Then walk to Nea Chora beach (12 min) for your first swim. The water is warm and calm.

Pallas — feet-in-the-sand fish taverna right at Nea Chora beach. Fresh catch grilled whole.

🌙 Evening

Sunset drinks on the harbour wall. Dinner at the waterfront — splurge on seafood.

Thalassino Ageri — harbour-side seafood, romantic table on the water. Order the grilled octopus.

Day 3 — Balos Lagoon & Gramvousa 🌊

Mon Aug 24 · The famous lagoon
🌞 Morning

Pick up rental car. Drive 1 hr west to Kissamos harbour. Take the Balos ferry (€20pp return, 1 hr). The lagoon appears as the boat rounds the cape — Caribbean-blue water, white sand, waist-deep for 200m. Or drive the dirt road to the Balos parking lot and hike down (30 min, steep, bring good shoes).

⛳️ Afternoon

Swim all day in that turquoise lagoon. Scramble up to Gramvousa fortress (if arriving by ferry) for 360° views — an old Venetian castle on a barren island. Pack lunch and loads of water — there’s nothing to buy at Balos.

🌙 Evening

Ferry back to Kissamos. Dinner on the harbour before driving back to Chania.

Papadakis — honest harbour taverna in Kissamos. Fresh fish, cold beer, €12.
Map · ~€14pp
~$80 (ferry + food + fuel) 🗺 Route map

Day 4 — Samaria Gorge ⛰️

Tue Aug 25 · Europe’s longest gorge
🌄 Morning

Return rental car. Take the 6:15 AM KTEL bus from Chania bus station to Omalos (1 hr, €8). Enter Samaria Gorge (€5 entry). The hike is 16 km, mostly downhill, through massive cliff walls, pine forests, and wild goat territory. The “Iron Gates” — where the gorge narrows to 3 metres wide — is unforgettable. Budget 5–7 hours.

🏞️ Afternoon

Emerge at Agia Roumeli on the Libyan Sea. Jump in the water — you’ve earned it. The beach here is pebbly but gorgeous. Grab a late lunch.

Livikon — post-hike feast at the Agia Roumeli waterfront. Huge portions, cold Mythos.
Map · ~€12pp
🌙 Evening

Ferry from Agia Roumeli to Hora Sfakion (€13, 1 hr — stunning coast views). KTEL bus back to Chania (2 hr, €8). Collapse into bed. Worth every step.

~$55 (bus + entry + ferry + food) 🗺 Route map

Day 5 — Elafonissi Beach 🏝️

Wed Aug 26 · Pink sand paradise
🌞 Morning

Pick up rental car again. Drive 75 min southwest to Elafonissi Beach. Pink-white sand, knee-deep turquoise lagoons, warm water. Wade across to the island side for more space. Bring an umbrella or rent one (€8). Arrive before 10 AM to beat the bus crowds.

🏞️ Afternoon

On the way back, stop at Chrysoskalitissa Monastery (10 min from Elafonissi) — whitewashed cliff-top monastery with sea views, free. Then continue to Falassarna Beach (30 min north) for the best sunset on Crete. Wide sandy beach, west-facing, the sun drops straight into the sea.

Sunset (Falassarna) — aptly named beach taverna. Grilled lamb chops, local wine, sunset included.
Map · ~€15pp
🌙 Evening

Drive back to Chania (50 min). Late night harbour stroll — the harbour bars stay lively till 2 AM.

💡 Quick Tips

Samaria Gorge prep: Gate opens 7 AM, last entry 1 PM. Start early to beat the heat. Bring 2L+ water, hat, sunscreen, sturdy shoes (not sandals). The last 3 km are flat and hot. The ferry from Agia Roumeli runs until ~6 PM.
Cash matters: Cards accepted at most Chania restaurants, but small tavernas in villages, gorge entrance, and beach vendors are cash-only. ATMs on every main street in Old Town. Wise card for best exchange rates.
Getting to Chania: From CHQ airport: taxi €25 (15 min) or KTEL city bus €2.50 (30 min). If flying via Athens: Aegean or Sky Express domestic flights are 45 min to CHQ, €50–80 one way.
Cretan food value: Crete has the best food-to-price ratio in Europe. Full taverna dinner with wine: €12–18pp. A gyro wrap is €3. Freddo espresso €2.50–3.50. Cretan olive oil is liquid gold — buy 1L at the market (€10) to take home.
