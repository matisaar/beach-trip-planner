🏠 Scala — Your Home Base

0 min from Airbnb

A tiny hilltop village — limited dining, but what's here is authentic and peaceful

Ristorante Zi'Ntonio

End of Via Torricella · Steps from your Airbnb · 📍 Map

The closest restaurant to "Windows on Heaven." Authentic Campanian cuisine in a family-run spot perched on the hillside. Known for traditional pasta dishes, local vegetables, and homemade limoncello. No frills — just proper Italian home cooking with a killer view.

💰 Budget: $$–$$$ · Expect €20–35/person for a full meal with wine.
★ Order this: Ask for whatever's fresh that day. Pasta with local vegetables, grilled meat, and finish with delizia al limone.

Da Lorenzo

Scala centro · ~5 min walk · 📍 Map

Another local gem in Scala's tiny piazza. Small, family-run trattoria serving classic Southern Italian dishes. Fewer tourists here than anywhere on the coast — you'll be eating with actual locals.

💰 Budget: $$–$$$ · Similar pricing, €20–30/person.
⚠ Heads up: Scala has very limited dining options. Most evenings you'll want to walk to Ravello (10 min) or drive/bus to Amalfi (15 min). No food delivery services reach Scala.

★ Ravello Must-Visits

Our top 3 recommendations for your week

Mimì Ristorante Pizzeria — The #1 restaurant in Ravello for good reason. 2,380 reviews, Travelers' Choice 2025. Their pizza dough is legendarily light ("like a cloud"). Start with the bruschetta and octopus salad, then a margherita or seafood pizza. €15–25/person. Go early — it gets packed.

Cumpa' Cosimo — The most-reviewed restaurant in Ravello (2,574 reviews). A Ravello institution since forever. Old-school Neapolitan/Campanian food. Try the bolognese, the veal with limoncello, and definitely the tiramisu. €20–35/person.

Babel Wine Bar Deli & Art — Perfect for a lighter evening. Great wine selection, charcuterie boards, and their lemon cake is incredible. More relaxed vibe than a full restaurant. Travelers' Choice 2025. €15–30/person.

💡 Local tip: Caseificio Staiano offers mozzarella-making classes in Ravello. 48 reviews, all raving. A unique Amalfi Coast experience — learn to make fresh mozzarella and eat it right there.

★ Amalfi Must-Visits

Our carefully curated picks — one for each budget

💰 Budget: Trattoria Rispoli (Pogerola, 10 min uphill from Amalfi) — The best-value restaurant on the entire coast. 443 reviews of people raving. A full 3-course meal with wine for around €25 each. Neapolitan home cooking in a hilltop setting with views. "Wine, antipasti, primo, secondo, dolce — around €50 for two."

🥚 Mid-Range: Ristorante L'Abside — 2,322 reviews, Travelers' Choice 2025. The sweet spot between quality and price. Their seafood pasta and homemade desserts are legendary. Lobster linguine is the standout. Beautiful atmospheric setting. €30–40/person with wine.

🌟 Splurge: Sensi Restaurant — 1 Michelin Star. The ultimate dinner experience on the coast. 6-course tasting menu executed with precision. Every dish is a work of art. Book ahead — small space, high demand. €120–150/person with wine pairing.

🍣 Street Food: Cuoppo d'Amalfi — A must-try Amalfi experience. Grab a paper cone of fresh-fried anchovies, calamari, and mixed seafood for €5–8. Perfect for a beachside lunch. 713 reviews.

⚠ Tourist trap warning: Restaurants directly facing the Duomo charge a 15–25% premium for the view alone. Walk one street back for the same quality at lower prices. Da Gemma (3,043 reviews) is a classic but reviewers note it's become "a little touristy."

★ Minori Must-Visits

Why you should make the 20 min trip at least once

Giardiniello — 2,886 reviews makes it the most-reviewed restaurant on this entire list. Great pizza, lobster linguini, fresh sea bass. Friendly service. €25–35/person.

Sal De Riso — Sal De Riso is THE celebrity pastry chef of the Amalfi Coast. His bistrot serves both savory and sweet — but you're really here for the desserts. His delizia al limone is world-famous. Book a table for lunch and save room for 2–3 desserts.

O' Sarracino — "Excellent pizza, excellent service, superb price." A no-frills pizza joint where locals eat. Margherita from €5. If you want a cheap Monday night dinner, this is it.

💡 Fun fact: Minori is the historic capital of pasta on the Amalfi Coast. The town's pasta-making tradition dates back over 800 years — fresh ndunderi (a local ricotta gnocchi) originated here.

🍝 Dishes You Must Try

The absolute essential Amalfi Coast foods — order these or regret it forever

🍝
Scialatielli ai Frutti di Mare
Thick handmade pasta ribbons with fresh clams, mussels, shrimp & squid in a light tomato/white wine sauce. THE Amalfi Coast pasta.
→ Best at: L'Abside, Da Ciccio, Taverna Buonvicino
🦞
Lobster Linguine
Fresh half-lobster over hand-cut linguine in a cherry tomato sauce. Rich, sweet, unforgettable. Expect €25–35 per plate.
→ Best at: Marina Grande, L'Abside, Giardiniello
🍣
Cuoppo di Mare
Paper cone of fresh-fried calamari, anchovies, shrimp & small fish. Street food perfection, €5–8.
→ Best at: Cuoppo d'Amalfi, Pescheria CICA, Pescheria Andrea's
🍋
Delizia al Limone
Sponge cake dome soaked in limoncello syrup, filled with lemon cream. The signature Amalfi dessert.
→ Best at: Sal De Riso, Pasticceria Gambardella, Babel
🍕
Pizza Napoletana
Wood-fired with San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella di bufala, basil. You're in the birthplace of pizza — eat it daily.
→ Best at: Mimì, Donna Stella, A Gradillo, O' Sarracino
🐟
Colatura di Alici
Anchovy sauce from nearby Cetara — liquid umami gold. Drizzled over spaghetti, it's an ancient Roman-era condiment still made today.
→ Best at: Any restaurant — ask for spaghetti con colatura
🧀
N'dunderi
Minori's local ricotta gnocchi — pillowy soft dumplings in a simple tomato sauce. An 800-year-old recipe only found here.
→ Best at: Giardiniello, La Botte, A Ricetta
🍦
Sfogliatella
Crispy, layered shell-shaped pastry filled with sweet ricotta & candied orange. Campania's beloved breakfast pastry.
→ Best at: Pasticceria Gambardella, Sal De Riso, any bar

📝 Practical Eating Tips

Know before you go — avoid the mistakes, eat like a local

Reservation Rules

August is peak season — plan ahead
  • Michelin restaurants: Book 2–4 weeks ahead. Sensi, La Caravella, Rossellinis, Glicine all fill up fast.
  • Popular mid-range: Book same-day or day before. Mimì, L'Abside, Giardiniello accept walk-ins but get crowded after 8pm.
  • Pizza places & trattorias: No reservation needed. Just show up. A Gradillo, O' Sarracino, Da Maria — first come, first served.

Eating Schedule (Italian Style)

Adjust to the local rhythm
  • Breakfast (7–10am): Stand at the bar. Espresso + cornetto (croissant). €2.50–4. Never cappuccino after 11am.
  • Lunch (12:30–2:30pm): Many restaurants close 3–7pm. Eat before 2pm or you're stuck with bars.
  • Aperitivo (6–8pm): Spritz or Campari with free snacks at a wine bar. Babel, Enotavola, or any terrace with a view.
  • Dinner (8–10:30pm): Italians eat late. Prime time 8:30–9:30pm. Don't show up at 6pm — you'll eat alone.

Money-Saving Tricks

Stretch your food budget further
  • Coperto: Cover charge (€2–4/person) is normal in Italy. Not a scam — just how it works.
  • House wine: Vino della casa is €4–8/carafe vs €20+ for bottled. It's usually excellent here.
  • Lunch menus: Some restaurants offer fixed-price lunch (pranzo fisso) for €12–18 — pasta + secondo + water.
  • Water: Ask for "acqua del rubinetto" (tap water) — it's free. Bottled is €2–3.
  • Skip the piazza: Restaurants with sea views or on main squares charge 20%+ more. Walk 30 seconds into a side street.
  • Aperitivo hack: A €8 Aperol Spritz often comes with free aperitivo snacks (olives, chips, bruschetta). It's basically a free light dinner.
