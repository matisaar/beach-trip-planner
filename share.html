Why Scala? Ancient hilltop village perched 400 m above the coast. 10 min walk down to Ravello, 15 min bus to Amalfi, 40 min bus to Positano. Quieter than the coast towns but right in the middle of everything. The views from the terrace are insane.

📍 The Neighbourhood

Everything around the house on Via Torricella, Scala

Bus stop at the door: There’s a SITA bus stop right at Ristorante Zi’Ntonio on Via Torricella — steps from the house. Buses run down to Amalfi (15 min) roughly every 30–60 min. From Amalfi you connect to everywhere on the coast.

🍕 Must-Try Food

The Amalfi Coast essentials

Dishes We Can’t Miss

Ask for these everywhere

Scialatielli ai frutti di mare — fresh hand-rolled pasta with seafood, the coast’s signature dish
Delizia al limone — lemon sponge cake with limoncello cream, heavenly
Frittura di paranza — crispy fried mixed fish, best at beach restaurants
Insalata caprese — local mozzarella di bufala + tomatoes, nothing like at home
Sfogliatella — crispy shell-shaped pastry filled with ricotta, a Neapolitan staple
Limoncello — homemade everywhere, served ice-cold after dinner (free at many spots)

Pro tip: Eat pranzo (lunch) as the big meal — many restaurants offer amazing set lunch menus. For dinner, local pizzerias are the move.

🚌 Getting Around

Buses, ferries & walking paths

By bus: SITA buses are the main way around the coast. Stop right at our door. Buy tickets at any tabaccheria (tobacco shop) — not on the bus. Runs every 30–60 min.

By foot to Ravello: 12 min downhill on ancient stone paths. The walk back up is ~18 min and steep, but shaded and scenic.

By ferry: Bus to Amalfi first, then ferries go to Positano (25 min), Salerno (35 min), or Capri (80 min).

Driving? Don’t. The coast road is a one-lane nightmare in August. Buses and ferries are the way.

🛵 Food Delivery & Eating In

No apps, but plenty of great options at the house

Can You Get Delivery in Scala?

Short answer: no app delivery. But we’ve got great options.

No delivery apps: Glovo, Just Eat, Deliveroo, and Uber Eats have zero coverage in Scala, Ravello, or Amalfi. These are tiny mountain villages with winding one-lane roads — none of the platforms operate here.

🍕 Takeaway pizza: Zi’Ntonio (1 min from the door!) does pizza da asporto — call or walk over, order, and bring it back to the terrace. Same with Sotto le Stelle and Il Pinguino. Just ask: “Pizza da portare via?”

🍳 Cook at home: The kitchen has everything. Grab fresh bread, mozzarella, local tomatoes, pasta & wine at Mini Market Mostaccioli (8 min walk). Eating on the terrace with that sea view is unbeatable.

🍲 Homemade breakfast: The host Cristina makes incredible homemade breakfast items — guests rave about her fig & lemon jam, French toast, and fresh fruit from the garden.

📞 Amalfi takeaway: If you’re in Amalfi town, Pizza Express (Via Pietro Capuano 46, ★ 4.6) does great takeaway pizza & calzones for the bus ride home.

💡 Quick Tips

SITA bus pass: You can get a 24-hr unlimited bus pass at any tabaccheria. Covers Scala–Amalfi, Amalfi–Positano, Amalfi–Ravello all in one.
Cash: Many small restaurants, buses, and beach vendors are cash-only. There’s an ATM in Amalfi at the post office.
Scala is our secret weapon: We’re above the chaos. The terrace gives us a sunset view every single night. Ravello is a 10-min walk. Amalfi is a 15-min bus. Best of both worlds.
