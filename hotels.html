🚚 Food Delivery — Same Problem Everywhere

We checked this previously — it’s the same whether you’re in a hotel or Airbnb

We already confirmed that Glovo, Just Eat, Deliveroo, and Uber Eats all have zero coverage in Scala, Ravello, and even most of Amalfi. This applies equally to hotels and Airbnbs — a hotel doesn’t fix this problem.

What a hotel does give you:

  • Breakfast is typically included (worth ~$15–20 CAD per person per day, saving ~$210–280)
  • Some have on-site restaurants for dinner (but at elevated hotel prices)
  • Front desk can call local restaurants for you

What the Airbnb gives you instead:

  • Full kitchen — cook whatever you want, whenever
  • Grocery store (Sigma) is a 5-min walk
  • Host provides breakfast supplies on arrival
  • Pizza da asporto from Zi’Ntonio in Scala (~€5–8)
  • More flexible schedule — eat when you want

🤔 When Would a Hotel Make Sense?

Being fair — here are some scenarios where a hotel might win

1. You hate cooking on vacation. If you genuinely don’t want to use a kitchen at all, a hotel with breakfast + on-site restaurant removes that decision. But you’d pay ~$1,500 more for the privilege.

2. You want a pool. The Airbnb has an incredible terrace but no pool. Hotels like Villa Fraulo ($580+/night) or Luna Convento ($830+/night) have pools. But that’s $3,000–5,000 extra for 7 nights.

3. Budget isn’t a factor. If you were working with a $10K+ budget, a stay at Hotel Caruso ($1,567/night) or Palazzo Avino ($933/night) would be phenomenal. But those are $7K–11K just for accommodation.

4. You prefer Amalfi town itself. If being in the centre of the action matters more than quiet and views, a B&B in Amalfi town puts you right there. But it’s still more expensive and gives you less space.

5. You’re a solo traveller. For one person, a hotel room makes more sense than renting a whole 2BR house. But for a couple, the Airbnb is perfect.

✅ Final Verdict

Stick with the Airbnb

For your specific situation — Toronto couple, $5K budget, 7 nights in late August — the Windows on Heaven Airbnb in Scala is the correct choice by every meaningful metric:

  • $1,091–1,541 cheaper than even the budget hotel option (including food costs)
  • 3x the living space of a hotel room
  • Full kitchen saves $400–500 on food over 7 days
  • Better rated — 4.98/5 vs 8.2/10 for Hotel Toro
  • Better location for your itinerary — between Scala and Ravello, quiet hilltop
  • Sea-view terrace that rivals any hotel in the area
  • Food delivery is nonexistent either way — hotel doesn’t fix that

The only thing a hotel adds is hands-free breakfast and (at higher price tiers) a pool. Those perks don’t justify an extra $1,500–5,000.

💸 Bottom line: The Airbnb saves you enough money to fund an extra day of vacation — or a proper splurge dinner at Palazzo Avino’s Michelin-starred restaurant instead ($100–150 per person).
