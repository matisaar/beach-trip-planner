🍕 Naples

EASY ADD-ON · 0 extra transport

Your flight lands at NAP. Stay 2–3 nights at the start or end — no extra bus/train needed.

Why Naples?

💰 Food: €5–15/meal 📍 93 entire homes under $100/night ✅ Safe in tourist areas

Naples is the birthplace of pizza and the cheapest major city in Southern Italy for food. A margherita from the legendary L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele costs €5. A full meal with wine runs €12–20/person. The historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Pompeii is a 30-min train ride. The Archaelogical Museum is world-class.

🌿 Puglia

BEST VALUE · 251 listings under $100/night

Italy's “heel” — baroque cities, trulli houses, stunning beaches, and food at half Amalfi prices

Why Puglia?

💰 Food: €8–20/meal 📍 251 homes from $74/night ✅ Very safe, friendly locals 🚆 3h train from Naples

Puglia is the anti-Amalfi Coast. Same incredible Italian food, same sea, same sunshine — but 40–60% cheaper and with a fraction of the tourists. Lecce is called the “Florence of the South” for its baroque architecture. Polignano a Mare has dramatic cliff-top sea views. The Trulli houses in Alberobello are a UNESCO site. Orecchiette pasta, burrata cheese, and focaccia Barese are staples — and a full dinner costs what an appetizer costs in Amalfi.

🌋 Sicily

CHEAPEST · 350 listings under $100/night

A whole different Italy — volcanoes, Greek ruins, the cheapest food in the country, and almost no tourist crowds inland

Why Sicily?

💰 Food: €4–15/meal 📍 350 homes from $57/night ✅ Safe for tourists ✈ $40–60 flight from NAP

Sicily is the cheapest region in Italy for both accommodation and food. A full sit-down lunch costs €8–12. Arancini from a street vendor cost €2. The island has more Greek temples than Greece, Mt Etna is Europe’s highest active volcano, and Catania’s fish market is one of the most cinematic places in all of Italy. Ragusa Ibla and Modica (Baroque Valley) are UNESCO World Heritage towns with almost zero international tourists. Catania has excellent nightlife, great food markets, and is the most “real” Italian city experience you can get.

🌊 Calabria

HIDDEN GEM · 49 listings under $100/night

Italy's best-kept secret — the toe of the boot, wild mountains, crystal waters, and virtually zero tourists

Why Calabria?

💰 Food: €6–18/meal 📍 49 homes from $82/night ✅ Safe, very welcoming 🚆 3–4h train from Naples

Calabria is where Italians go when they want cheap beach vacations. The region is remarkably untouched by international tourism — you’ll barely hear English spoken. The Tyrrhenian coast (Tropea, Pizzo) has turquoise water rivaling the Amalfi Coast. Cosenza has a charming old town. The food is fiery (nduja, pepperoncino in everything) and absurdly cheap. Think of it as the raw, authentic Italy before the Instagram crowds arrived.

🏙 Cilento Coast & Paestum

CLOSEST OPTION · 1h south of Amalfi

Like the Amalfi Coast 30 years ago — same beauty, 1/3 the price, almost no international tourists

Why Cilento?

💰 Food: €8–20/meal 📍 105 homes from $79/night ✅ Very safe, National Park 🚌 1h from Amalfi

The Cilento National Park (UNESCO) is just an hour south of the Amalfi Coast but feels like a different world. Paestum has 2,500-year-old Greek temples that rival the Parthenon. Castellabate (“Welcome to the South” filming location) is a gorgeous hilltop town with beaches below. Agropoli, Acciaroli, and Marina di Camerota have crystal-clear water with none of the Amalfi crowds. Best of all — you can combine it with your Amalfi stay without flying anywhere.

🏛 Matera (Basilicata)

UNIQUE EXPERIENCE · Cave city

Europe's most extraordinary town — cave dwellings, 9,000 years of history, “the Sassi”

Why Matera?

💰 Food: €8–20/meal 📍 Cave hotels from $93/night ✅ Extremely safe 🚆 4h from Naples

Matera’s Sassi (ancient cave dwellings carved into limestone cliffs) are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most photographed places in Italy. It was the European Capital of Culture in 2019 and served as the filming location for recent James Bond and biblical films. The town went from being “the shame of Italy” (it was evacuated in the 1950s due to poverty) to one of its most stunning destinations. Many Airbnbs here are actual converted caves. Food is hearty Basilicatan fare — pasta with peppers, crusco peppers, lamb, and incredible local bread.

🔒 Safety Overview

All regions recommended here are safe for tourists — here's what to know

💛
Naples
Safe in tourist areas

Avoid dimly-lit back streets at night. Spaccanapoli & waterfront are fine. Petty theft—keep valuables close.

💚
Puglia
Very safe

One of Italy’s safest regions. Small towns are extremely welcoming. Zero violent crime concerns.

💚
Sicily
Safe for tourists

Mafia stereotypes are outdated. Tourist areas are safe. Catania can be gritty but no worse than any big city.

💚
Calabria
Very safe for tourists

Extremely welcoming locals. Tourist infrastructure is less developed but crime against visitors is essentially zero.

💚
Cilento
Extremely safe

National park region, mostly Italian families on holiday. Peaceful, relaxed, zero safety concerns.

💚
Matera
Extremely safe

Small UNESCO city, walkable, well-lit. One of the safest places in all of Southern Italy.

⚠ General Southern Italy safety: Keep your phone in a front pocket in crowded areas. Don’t leave bags unattended on beaches. Use official taxis or Uber/FreeNow apps. Lock your rental car and don’t leave valuables visible. These are the same precautions you’d take in any European city.

🎯 Our Recommendation

If we could only pick one extension option

💚 Add 2–3 Nights in Naples + 2 Nights in Matera

Total trip: 11–12 nights · Extra cost: ~$650–$800 CAD

Start in Naples for the world’s best pizza, Pompeii, and the National Archaeological Museum. Then bus/drive to the Amalfi Coast for your 7 nights in Scala. After checkout, head to Matera for 2 nights in a literal cave dwelling. It’s on the way to the airport (roughly) and makes for an unforgettable finale. Train back to Naples for your flight home.

This gives you three completely different Italian experiences: chaotic, foodie Naples → scenic, romantic Amalfi Coast → ancient, otherworldly Matera. All for under $6,100 CAD total.
