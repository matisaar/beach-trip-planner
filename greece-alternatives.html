Other Greek Islands

We picked Chania, but here’s what else was in the running — and how to extend the trip

✔️ Verdict: Chania wins for this trip

Best food scene, best beaches, best value, and the most variety for an 8-day first visit. But these are worth knowing about for future trips or a quick extension.

🏜️ The Cyclades

The classic Greek island postcard — white-blue villages, volcanic sunsets

Santorini (Thira) ICONIC $$$

Oia sunsets, caldera views, volcanic beaches, wine tasting
✈️ Flight: ~$1,900/pp from YYZ (via ATH)
🏠 Hotels: $200–800/night
🍴 Meals: $25–60/pp
Why consider: Bucket-list destination. Oia sunset is legendary. Wine from volcanic grapes. Caldera swimming. Red Beach, Perissa black sand beach. Incredible photography.
Why we didn’t pick it: Massively overpriced in August ($300+/night minimums). Overcrowded — cruise ships dump 10,000 people/day. Very small, limited to 3–4 days of activities. Food is mediocre-to-good at high prices. Not enough variety for 7 nights. Budget: would blow past $7K easily.

Naxos HIDDEN GEM $$

Largest Cycladic island — great beaches, mountain villages, local food
✈️ Flight: ~$1,800/pp (via ATH + domestic)
🏠 Hotels: $80–180/night
🍴 Meals: $15–30/pp
Why consider: Best beaches in the Cyclades (Plaka, Agios Prokopios). Excellent local cuisine — Naxian cheese and potatoes are legendary. Mountain villages (Halki, Apiranthos). Portara temple gate at sunset. Way less touristy than Santorini/Mykonos. Ferry connections to Paros, Santorini, Ios.
Why we didn’t pick it: Less variety than Crete — no gorges, fewer world-class beaches. Hard to fill 7 full days without island-hopping. Still pricier than Crete.

Milos HIDDEN GEM $$

Volcanic island, 70+ beaches, Kleftiko sea caves, Sarakiniko lunar beach
✈️ Flight: ~$1,900/pp (via ATH + domestic)
🏠 Hotels: $100–250/night
🍴 Meals: $18–35/pp
Why consider: Sarakiniko (white moonscape beach) is otherworldly. Kleftiko sea caves by boat. Fishing village of Klima with colourful boat garages. 70+ beaches to explore. Venus de Milo was found here. Emerging but still uncrowded.
Why we didn’t pick it: Small island — 3–4 days max. August accommodation books up fast and prices spike. Need a car, limited public transit. No major towns for evening walks.

Paros POPULAR $$

Charming Naoussa, golden beaches, windsurfing, Cycladic architecture

🏝️ Dodecanese & Ionian Islands

Beyond the Cyclades — medieval towns, lush green, Italian influence

Rhodes POPULAR $$

Medieval Old Town (UNESCO), ancient ruins, Lindos acropolis, long beaches
✈️ Flight: ~$1,700/pp (via ATH or IST)
🏠 Hotels: $70–180/night
🍴 Meals: $12–28/pp
Why consider: Rhodes Old Town is one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe. Lindos acropolis with that insane view. Long sandy beaches on the east coast. Good value for money. Direct flights from many European hubs. Warm water in August (27°C).
Why we didn’t pick it: Can be quite resort-touristy outside Old Town. Beaches are nice but don’t compete with Balos/Elafonissi. Food scene is decent but not Chania-level.

Corfu (Kerkyra) UNDERRATED $$

Venetian Old Town (UNESCO), emerald water, lush green, Italian influence
✈️ Flight: ~$1,700/pp (via ATH or European hub)
🏠 Hotels: $70–160/night
🍴 Meals: $12–28/pp
Why consider: Venetian, French, and British architectural layers. Stunning emerald/turquoise coves. Lush green unlike most Greek islands. Canal d’Amour. Paleokastritsa monastery and bays. UNESCO Old Town with Italian feel. Good base for day trips to Albania and Paxos/Antipaxos.
Why we didn’t pick it: Less “Greek island” feeling. Beaches can’t match Crete’s world-famous ones. More rain in August than Crete (still rare). Food is good but not exceptional.

🇪️ Alternative Crete Bases

Same island, different vibe — if Chania doesn’t click

Rethymno STRONG ALT $$

Smaller than Chania, equally charming, Venetian/Ottoman Old Town, Fortezza castle
🏠 Airbnb: $100–140/night
🍴 Meals: $12–25/pp
🚗 From CHQ: 1h bus
Why consider: Even cheaper than Chania. Fortezza is incredible at sunset. Long sandy beach in town. Excellent restaurants (Avli, Prima Plora). Less touristy, more local feel. Closer to Preveli and Plakias beaches. Well-connected by KTEL bus.
Why Chania is better: Chania has a bigger harbour, more restaurant variety, closer to Balos/Elafonissi/Samaria, and the Venetian Quarter is more atmospheric.

Heraklion FOR HISTORY BUFFS

Crete’s capital, Knossos Palace, Archaeological Museum, urban energy
🏠 Airbnb: $80–130/night
🍴 Meals: $10–22/pp
🚗 From CHQ: 2.5h bus
Why consider: Knossos Palace — the Minoan civilization’s heart. World-class Archaeological Museum. Cheapest accommodation in Crete. More flights to HER than CHQ. Good central base for day trips east (Agios Nikolaos, Spinalonga, Vai).
Why Chania is better: Heraklion city is not charming — it’s a working city, not a vacation destination. Far from western Crete’s best beaches. You can visit Knossos as a day trip from Chania if you want.

Agios Nikolaos CHILL OPTION

Eastern Crete lakeside town, Spinalonga fortress, palm beach at Vai
🏠 Airbnb: $80–120/night
🍴 Meals: $10–22/pp
🚗 From HER: 1h drive
Why consider: Lake Voulismeni in the town centre. Spinalonga island (Venetian fortress, leper colony — haunting history). Vai palm beach (Europe’s largest natural palm forest). Elounda luxury strip. Very chill, less touristic than Chania.
Why Chania is better: Much less restaurant/nightlife variety. Eastern Crete beaches are nice but not world-class like Balos/Elafonissi. Far from Samaria Gorge. Harder to get to from Toronto (fly to HER then drive).

🌊 Crete’s South Coast

Wild, remote, breathtaking — if you want the adventurous version of this trip

Loutro HIDDEN GEM $

No roads in. Only by boat or hiking. Tiny fishing village on the Libyan Sea.
🚢 Access: Ferry from Hora Sfakion or hike from Samaria
🏠 Rooms: $60–100/night
🍴 Meals: $10–18/pp
Only accessible by boat or on foot — this is what Greek islands felt like 30 years ago. Crystal-clear water, whitewashed buildings, zero cars, zero noise. Kayak to Sweetwater Beach (Maps). Hike to Marmara Beach. Perfect for 2–3 nights of total disconnection.

Plakias & Preveli $

Laid-back beach town + palm forest river beach
🚗 From Rethymno: 35 min
🏠 Rooms: $50–90/night
🍴 Meals: $8–15/pp
Plakias has a long sandy beach, good tavernas, and a relaxed hippie feel. Preveli Beach (Maps) has a river lined with palm trees flowing into the sea — otherworldly. Damnoni Beach nearby is excellent. Good for budget-minded travellers.

Matala HIPPIE LEGEND

Beach caves where Joni Mitchell lived, south coast sunsets, Red Beach hike
🚗 From Heraklion: 1.5h
🏠 Rooms: $50–80/night
🍴 Meals: $8–15/pp
Famous caves in the cliff where hippies lived in the 1960s–70s (Joni Mitchell wrote “Carey” here). Red Beach is a short hike over the headland. Annual beach festival in June. Very relaxed, cheap, and authentically weird. Not for everyone, but memorable.

🏛️ Athens Stopover

If your flight connects through Athens, add a night — it’s worth it

Athens in 24 Hours EASY ADD-ON

Acropolis, Plaka, rooftop bars, incredible street food
🏠 Hotels: $80–150/night (Plaka/Monastiraki)
🍴 Meals: $10–20/pp
✈️ To CHQ: 45 min Aegean flight
Morning: Acropolis at opening (8 AM, €20pp) → Acropolis Museum (€10pp). Lunch: Souvlaki at Kostas in Syntagma (€3.50) or meze at Karamanlidika (€15pp). Afternoon: Walk through Plaka → Anafiotika (mini Cyclades neighbourhood!) → Monastiraki flea market. Sunset: Rooftop cocktails at A for Athens or Couleur Locale overlooking the Acropolis. Dinner: Taverna in Psyrri (Oineas, Avocado).
